<p><p>The water corrosion inhibitor market is broadly categorized into two types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic inhibitors, such as phosphates and chromates, work by creating protective layers on metal surfaces, reducing corrosion rates. They are often used in industrial applications due to their effectiveness and cost-efficiency. Organic inhibitors, on the other hand, utilize organic compounds like amines and surfactants to prevent corrosion by adsorbing onto metal surfaces. These are favored in systems requiring lower toxicity and environmental impact, such as in cooling water systems.</p></p>
