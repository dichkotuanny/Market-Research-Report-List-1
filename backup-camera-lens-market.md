<p><p>The backup camera lens market primarily features two types of imaging sensors: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras. CCD cameras are renowned for their superior image quality and sensitivity, making them suitable for low-light conditions. In contrast, CMOS cameras are favored for their low power consumption, faster processing speeds, and cost efficiency, allowing for integration into modern vehicles. Both types cater to the growing demand for enhanced safety and driver assistance in automotive applications.</p></p>
