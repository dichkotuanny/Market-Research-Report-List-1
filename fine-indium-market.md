<p><ul><li>Native Indium</li><li>Regenerated Indium</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fine indium market comprises two primary types: native indium and regenerated indium. Native indium is sourced directly from natural ore deposits, characterized by its high purity and quality, making it ideal for specialized applications, particularly in electronics and solar cells. Regenerated indium, on the other hand, is produced through the recycling of indium-containing materials, such as discarded electronics. This type offers a more sustainable and cost-effective alternative while meeting demand, particularly as environmental concerns drive the emphasis on recycling and resource conservation.</p></p>
