<p><p>Hydrophobic fumed silica is categorized into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I is primarily used as a thickening agent in various applications, including coatings and adhesives, due to its high surface area and low density. Type II, on the other hand, is designed for enhancing flowability and reducing clumping in powdered products. Both types are crucial in industries like paints, plastics, and pharmaceuticals, offering unique properties that optimize performance and stability in formulations.</p></p>
