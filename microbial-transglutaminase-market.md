<p><p>Microbial Transglutaminase (MTG) is an enzyme that catalyzes the formation of cross-links between proteins, enhancing textural properties and improving the structural integrity of food products. It is derived from microbial sources, making it a cost-effective alternative to the animal-derived transglutaminase. Primarily used in the food industry, MTG serves various applications including meat processing, dairy products, and plant-based foods, where it aids in binding and improving texture.</p><p>The Microbial Transglutaminase Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.6% during the forecast period, driven by an increasing demand for natural and clean-label products. Consumers' health consciousness and the trend towards plant-based diets are contributing to the rise in MTG utilization in food formulations. Additionally, the growing adoption of advanced food processing techniques is pushing the boundaries for innovative uses of MTG, particularly in enhancing food texture and quality. The rise of the food processing industry, along with a focus on reducing food waste through better product yield, further supports market expansion.
